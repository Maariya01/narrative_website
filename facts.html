Space Facts

Things you may not have known about Space

Sun and Moon

The Sun is 400 times larger than the Moon, but also 400 times as far away, making both objects appear to be the same size in our sky.

Space and Humans

Space is a vacuum, the presence of oxygen is nearly non-existent, and the air pressure is dangerously low. Without the protection of suitable spacesuits, humans would essentially implode due to the air being drawn out from their bodies.

Speed of Light

Light travels at a constant speed of 1,079,252,848.8 (1.07 billion) km per hour. That works out to 299,792,458 m/s, or about 670,616,629 mph (miles per hour).

To provide perspective, consider that when you gaze at the stars, you're essentially witnessing them as they appeared in the past when their light finally reaches our eyes. The intriguing aspect is that by the time we observe certain stars, they might have even ceased to exist. ex quae? Nihil, dolorem!

Neuron star

Neuron stars are so dense, one teaspoon of its matter would weigh over a trillion kilograms, exceeding the weight of the entire human population.

White holes

While black holes are known for absorbing matter and light, white holes, on the other hand, emit matter and light

Andromeda

The Andromeda galaxy is the closest galaxy to our Milky way and it can be see with the naked eye

Neptune has only completed one orbit since its discovery

Earth is 149,598,262 km from the sun and takes 365.24 days to complete an orbit. Neptune is 4,498,396,441 km from the sun and takes 164.8 Earth years to complete one orbit.

Void in Space

Sound cannot travel through a vacuum and so space is completely silent

Earth's Speed

Earth is currently moving at a speed of 30 kilometres per second. We do not feel as it is moving at a constant speed

Europa

Jupiter’s moon, Europa could harbour extraterrestrial life as there is assumption that the planet has a giant ocean beneath its crust

Infinite Stars

The amount of stars in the universe is unknown, the estimation is around 70 sextillion, we know that in our milky way approximately 100 billion stars.

The Red Planet

Mars earn its name, the Red planet due to its distinctive red appearance, the is due the presence of iron oxide commonly known as rust

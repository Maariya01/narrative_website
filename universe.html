The Milky Way

The Universe encompasses everything within our current knowledge and the vast mysteries awaiting discovery. It comprises planets, galaxies, and other phenomena. The Milky way Galaxy is just one of many billion other galaxies. When contemplating Earth from the immense scale of the Milky Way, our home appears as a minuscule dot in comparison to the magnificence of the infinite universe.

How far does the Universe go?

The vast expanse of the Universe poses a challenge for astronomers, as determining its edge remains unknown, making it impossible to fully explore. Despite advancements in technology, what lies beyond the observable universe remains a mystery, leaving science with only more questions.

Dark matter

Dark matter constitutes approximately 27% of the universe; however, its composition remains largely unknown. Characterised by its elusive, undetectable nature, dark matter proves indispensable and exhibits resistance to all known forces known to man such as light and gravitational forces.

Black holes

There are two known ways in which black hole are formed. The first, known as the 'Stellar' method, occurs when a star, approximately 20 times more massive than the sun, collapses under its own gravity during its dying stage, resulting in the creation of a black hole. The second process traces back to the early universe, if explored further, this proposition can answer many other questions such as the formation of dark matter

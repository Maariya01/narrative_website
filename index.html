James Webb Telescope

NGC 5584.

A mesmerising spiral galaxy situated in the constellation Virgo, approximately 72 million light-years away from Earth. NGC 5584 has these ‘spiral arms’ filled with bright, ‘young star’ groups. You can see thin dark streaks of dust that seem to flow from the yellowish centre, within this region, the older stars reside. The red dots that are visible indicate other surrounding galaxies.

HH 211

It consits of clouds and dust which is the way in which stars form. This star is 1000 lightyears away from earth, this means that the image we see, is how it was 1000 years ago. Objects like HH 211 helps us learn about how stars form early on and how material is ejected from young star systems. Observing HH 211 adds to our understanding of how gravity and magnetic fields interact in areas where stars are being formed.
